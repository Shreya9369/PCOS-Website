Understanding PCOS

PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) is a common hormonal disorder that affects people with ovaries, often during their reproductive years. It is characterized by irregular menstrual cycles, high levels of male hormones (androgens), and the development of cysts in the ovaries. Symptoms can include irregular periods, acne, excessive hair growth (hirsutism), and weight gain. PCOS can also affect fertility, as it may prevent the ovaries from releasing eggs regularly. The exact cause of PCOS is not fully understood, but it is believed to be influenced by genetic and environmental factors. Insulin resistance is common in people with PCOS, which can lead to increased levels of insulin and further hormone imbalances. Managing PCOS typically involves lifestyle changes, such as a healthy diet and regular exercise, along with medical treatments like hormonal contraceptives, anti-androgens, and medications to manage insulin levels.

Why PCOS Awareness Matters

PCOS can cause irregular periods, acne, weight gain, excessive hair growth, and even infertility. It also increases the risk of developing conditions like type 2 diabetes, heart disease, and depression.

Raising awareness and providing accessible tools for early detection and lifestyle management is crucial. Our goal is to support women with PCOS by providing knowledge, resources, and a community to thrive in.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can PCOS be cured?

There is no cure, but symptoms can be managed effectively through lifestyle changes and medical care.

Does everyone with PCOS have cysts?

No, not everyone with PCOS develops ovarian cysts. The name is a bit misleading!
